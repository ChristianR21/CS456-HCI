Bad Example 1: Car Infotainment System

The purpose of this interface is control certain functions of a car, that don’t relate with the actual driving the vehicle. It is an overall bad design as it can be difficult for someone to learn what everything does, and where to find it. Especially if you are driving this vehicle for the first time and are trying to change the song or change the temperature while driving down the highway.

Image:

I think it was designed this way to make it both appeasing to the eye yet, easy to reach all the buttons. However, it resulted in an overly cumbersome interface that is hard to figure out to use. A better design would have been move the display in between the audio buttons and the heating/cooling buttons. Another improvement would be by moving the lock and unlock to the driver side.

Good Example 1: Vornado Heater/Fan

- The purpose of the overall interface is to control the heater’s heating functionality. I find the interface to be quite well. For one, the heater can be powered on by pressing the universally recognized power icon (which is always lit) on either the unit itself or via the remote control.

images

Once powered on, the display lights up and the heater starts on the last set setting. The display stays on until the heater is turned off, and is clear enough and bright to read from a distance. The display also displays text of the current room temperature is and what the set temperature is. he accompanying icons are recognizable and easy to figure out they do, and the display updates immediately after input. Perhaps the biggest downside of the interface is the lack of physical buttons, so someone with no vision, will have a difficult time figuring out how to change the settings. I’m not sure why it was designed that way. I know that with physical buttons, they tend to become sticky over time or just no longer work, whereas capacitive buttons last a long and don’t wear out the way physical ones do. However, I’d imagine that it is rather easy to add some braille near the buttons so that blind people can still understand what the buttons do.


Good Example 2: Smartphone Smart Launcher 5

Smart Launcher 5 is an overall good interface for smartphones as it allows users to access all their apps, widgets, time, reminders, and current local weather, in a fast, simple to use way. It’s particular good in the way that organizes everything. For example, on the main page, you can see the current time, current weather, reminders for today, most used apps with the amount of notifications, and a smart search bar at the bottom. Swiping to left takes you to your widget pages, swiping to the right opens the news, swiping up opens your app drawer, and swiping down opens the smart search along with the keyboard. I think it was designed this way to declutter the hundreds of apps on smartphones and to help users find certain apps or information quickly.
